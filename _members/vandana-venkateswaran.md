Vandana was born in Chennai, India and completed her PhD in Dr. Chaitanya S. Gokhale's Research Group for Theoretical Models in Eco-Evolutionary dynamics at the Max Planck Institute for Evolutionary Biology.

## Background

She holds a BSc in Physics from Delhi University and an MSc in Nuclear Fusion and Engineering Physics from Ghent University, Belgium through an Erasmus Mundus programme. Her interdisciplinary background in computational physics and theoretical biology led her to pursue research in evolutionary dynamics.

## Research Focus

Vandana's research concentrated on understanding interactions between biological entities using evolutionary game theory. Her work examined how individuals engage in multiple interactions (or games) simultaneously and how this impacts fitness and evolutionary dynamics. She was particularly interested in life-history evolution, studying the relationship between parental investment and immune response in various species. She also investigated clutch-splitting strategies in fish.

## Research Interests
