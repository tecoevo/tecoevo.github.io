Prateek grew up in Shivpuri, a small town in India, and completed an integrated BS-MS degree in Physics from IISER-Kolkata. During his studies, he became especially interested in evolutionary dynamics and the application of mathematical techniques for understanding complex social and biological phenomena at different scales.

## PhD Research

Prateek completed his PhD at IISER-Kolkata where he analyzed evolutionary game theoretical models of harassment bribery and bacterial competition involving antibiotic producer, sensitive, and degrader cells in spatial environments. Both research topics were motivated by current societal issues.

## Postdoctoral Focus

As a postdoc, Prateek focused on understanding synthetic gene drive systems. His research assessed the risks of synthetic gene-drive systems in terms of spread and resistance evolution using mathematical modelling and simulations. Synthetic gene drives are genetic engineering technologies that bias the inheritance of desired genes in populations, with applications in human health, agriculture, and conservation, but also significant risks that require careful analysis.

## Notable Tool Development

Prateek developed DrMxR (Drive Mixer), a handy online tool to explore the population-level consequences of different drive systems, available at https://pverma.shinyapps.io/DrMxR/
